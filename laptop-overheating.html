Common causes

Common causes of overheating

Dust, blocked vents, soft surfaces, heavy apps, gaming, and poor airflow are the most common reasons a laptop gets hotter than normal.

Safe first steps

Safe fixes to try first

  1. Use a flat surface. A desk or table helps air move through the vents.
  2. Clean vents gently. Remove visible dust from vents without pushing debris deeper inside.
  3. Close heavy apps. Games, video editors, and many browser tabs can raise temperatures fast.
  4. Restart laptop. This clears stuck background tasks that may be heating the system.

Avoid damage

What not to do

Do not block vents, ignore repeated shutdowns, or open the laptop without the right knowledge. If heat problems keep returning, it may need cleaning or repair.

FAQ

Why is my laptop overheating?

Common causes include dust, blocked vents, soft surfaces, heavy apps, gaming, poor airflow, or cooling parts that are struggling.

Can overheating slow my laptop?

Yes. When a laptop gets too hot, it may reduce performance to protect internal parts, which can make apps and Windows feel slow.

Is it safe to use a hot laptop?

Warm is normal, but very hot surfaces, shutdowns, or burning smells are warning signs. Stop heavy use and let the laptop cool.

When should I get it repaired?

Get repair help if the laptop shuts down from heat, the fan sounds abnormal, vents stay hot after cleaning, or overheating keeps returning.
